## ğŸ“œ QUALITY FRAMEWORK (Source: Quality Framework - Notion - Main.pdf)

### 1. Translation Errors
*Definition*: Translation issues that may impair the conveying of the original message.
- **Addition**: Information not in source is added, distorting meaning.
- **Omission**: Essential information is missing.
- **Mistranslation**: Does not accurately represent source.
- **Untranslated**: Content left in source language.

*Examples*:
- **Minor (0.5)**: "San Francisco" -> "San Francisco, the vibrant heart..." (Addition). Missing "local" in a list (Omission).
- **Major (2.0)**: "Awaiting payment" -> "Payment not complete" (Mistranslation). Japanese sentence left in English text (Untranslated).

### 2. Terminology and Consistency
*Definition*: Violation of company guidelines or Notion glossary.
- **Minor (0.5)**: "FAQ" translated correctly but differently from glossary.
- **Major (2.0)**: "Manage My Listings" translated differently than the UI button.

### 3. Language Quality
*Definition*: Issues proven by official linguistic sources (Punctuation, Spelling, Grammar).
- **Minor (0.5)**: Semicolon used instead of comma. Typo in a word.
- **Major (2.0)**: Misplaced comma changes meaning. Offensive typo. Wrong date format (MM/DD/YYYY in France).

### 4. Style
*Definition*: Tone, register, or flow not suitable for target.
- **Minor (0.5)**: Style not appropriate for FAQ. Literal translation of idioms.
- **Major (2.0)**: Formal tone used instead of informal (or vice versa). "Roll with butter" (literal idiom).

### 5. Tag Issues
*Definition*: Tags (e.g., <ph>) omitted, duplicated, or misplaced.
- **Minor (0.5)**: Tag issue that doesn't break the UI.
- **Major (2.0)**: Tag issue that breaks layout or functionality.
