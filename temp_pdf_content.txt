Translation ErrorsTranslation issues that may impair the conveying of the original message. 
Addition: Translation includes the information that is not present in the source, and the addition distorts the meaning of the message, does not fit with the text style and tone or overall context of the message.Omission: Translation is missing the information that is present in the source, and the missing part is essential to render the meaning and the tone of the original message.Mistranslation: Translation does not accurately represent the source content.Untranslated: Content that should have been translated has been left untranslated.

Minor (0.5 error point): Error that does not confuse the user or lead to loss of meaning, but may be noticable for the user and creates a target that falls short of linguistic and stylistic quality expectations. Such error propagates 0.5 error point.Major (2 error points): The message meaning is changed or it is hard to comprehend; the message confuses the user; might lead to an unintended action, sending a query to customer support or even stopping using the product. A major error propagates 2 error points.
Repeated Errors & Neutral Edits- Neutral edit: Translation is in line with the Notion Quality Framework, meaning, there is no quantifiable error and the translation is already fluent and natural, but the reviewer refines the translation style even further. - Repeat: A repeated error is the repeated occurence of a single error. Please note that typos should not be seen as repeated errors.
